## ğŸ“‹ HIRING PROCESS

### **Step 1: Write Job Description**

**Title:** "Designer for Tech Agency (Part-Time / Freelance)"

**Description:**
```
We're Zumetrix Labs, a software development agency serving international
clients. We build SaaS MVPs, mobile apps, and AI automation solutions.

We need a designer to handle:
- Client case studies and portfolio visuals
- Social media graphics
- Marketing materials
- Client-facing documents

This is a part-time/freelance role with potential to grow.

Requirements:
- Portfolio showing professional, modern design work
- Experience with Figma and Adobe Creative Suite
- Understanding of tech/SaaS industry aesthetics
- Self-sufficient and deadline-driven

To Apply:
- Share your portfolio
- Tell us about a project you're proud of
- Include your rate and availability
```

---

### **Step 2: Screen Candidates**

**Review Portfolio:**
- Is the work professional and modern?
- Does it fit tech/SaaS aesthetic?
- Is there variety in the work?

**Initial Questions:**
1. What design tools do you use?
2. What's your process for a new project?
3. How do you handle feedback and revisions?
4. What's your availability and turnaround time?

**Shortlist:** 3-5 candidates

---

### **Step 3: Test Project**

**Give a Small Paid Test:**
"Design a case study page for one of our projects"

**Provide:**
- Project details
- Brand colors and fonts
- Inspiration examples
- Clear deadline (3-5 days)

**Pay:** $50-$100 for test project

**Evaluate:**
- Quality of work
- Communication during process
- Turnaround time
- How they handled feedback

---

### **Step 4: Make Decision**

**Criteria:**
- Best design quality
- Best communication
- Most reliable
- Fair pricing

**Hire:** 1 designer (don't split work between multiple designers)

## ğŸš¨ COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

### **âŒ Mistake #1: No Clear Brief**
Designer guesses what you want, wastes time on wrong direction.

**Solution:** Use project request template every time.

---

### **âŒ Mistake #2: Too Many Revisions**
"Can you try this... and this... and this..."

**Solution:** Limit to 2 rounds of revisions. Get it right in the brief.

---

### **âŒ Mistake #3: Slow Feedback**
Designer delivers, you take 1 week to review.

**Solution:** Review and respond within 24-48 hours.

---

### **âŒ Mistake #4: Micromanaging**
"Move this 2 pixels left"

**Solution:** Trust their expertise. Focus on outcomes, not details.

---

### **âŒ Mistake #5: Scope Creep**
"Can you also design this, and this, and this..."

**Solution:** Stick to original brief. New requests = new projects.

---

## ğŸ“ WORKING WITH DESIGNERS: BEST PRACTICES

### **âœ… DO:**

1. **Provide Clear Briefs**
   - What you need
   - Why you need it
   - Who it's for
   - When you need it

2. **Give Context**
   - Business goals
   - Target audience
   - Brand guidelines

3. **Share Examples**
   - "Something like this"
   - Inspiration references
   - What you like/don't like

4. **Respect Their Time**
   - Pay promptly
   - Respond quickly
   - Don't change scope mid-project

5. **Build Relationship**
   - Regular work
   - Fair pay
   - Positive feedback

---

### **âŒ DON'T:**

1. **Be Vague**
   - "Make it pop"
   - "Can you make it better?"
   - "I'll know it when I see it"

2. **Change Your Mind Constantly**
   - Wastes time
   - Frustrates designer
   - Costs more

3. **Expect Mind Reading**
   - If you have a vision, communicate it
   - Don't assume they'll guess correctly

4. **Compare to Unrealistic Standards**
   - "Make it look like Apple"
   - "Can you do it like [expensive agency]?"

5. **Delay Payment**
   - Pay on time, every time
   - Good designers are worth it
