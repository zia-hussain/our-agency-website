This is what happened next.

## Projects That Defined Our Year

### **1. Ifyify ‚Äì The AI Branding Tool**

**Client:** Kelly Andrews, Best Business Services (United States)
**Challenge:** Build a sophisticated AI-powered personal branding platform from scratch in 2 weeks

This was the project that showed us what we were capable of.

Kelly had a clear vision: a tool that could help professionals craft perfect bios, taglines, and brand personas using AI. But it needed to be more than functional‚Äîit needed to feel premium, intuitive, and fast.

We built the entire MVP in React with custom OpenAI integrations, animated style selectors, and a Supabase backend‚Äîall in 14 days.

The result? A live product that Kelly immediately started using with real clients. And a testimonial that still makes us proud: "Exceptional work ethic, committed to long-term collaboration."

**What we learned:** Fast doesn't mean sloppy. With the right architecture and focus, you can deliver quality at speed.

---

### **2. Forlag ‚Äì The Publishing Analytics Dashboard**

**Client:** Harald & Eivind, Norwegian Publishing Company
**Challenge:** Turn messy Excel workflows into a beautiful, functional analytics dashboard

This project taught us the value of understanding the client's actual problem‚Äînot just their technical requirements.

The client wasn't looking for fancy features. They needed **clarity**. They were drowning in spreadsheets, unable to see trends, risks, or opportunities in their sales and inventory data.

We built a React dashboard that ingests raw CSV files, computes metrics on the fly, and presents everything in a clean, intuitive interface. MTD/YTD comparisons. Inventory health. Dead stock alerts. All without needing a complex backend.

The testimonial said it all: "The dashboard gives us a clear, real-time picture of our business. It feels tailored for us, not like a generic off-the-shelf tool."

**What we learned:** Sometimes the best solution isn't the most technically impressive one. It's the one that solves the client's actual problem.

---

### **3. Floating Stone Ranch ‚Äì The Logistics Engine**

**Client:** James, Beef Processing Company (Canada)
**Challenge:** Digitize a complex beef processing workflow that had been running on paper and spreadsheets

This was one of the most unique projects we've worked on‚Äîand a reminder that software development isn't just for SaaS startups.

James needed a system to track cattle, carcasses, boxes, and shipments through a multi-step processing pipeline. It had to be reliable, structured, and scalable.

We designed an Airtable base with Make.com automation that transformed their entire operation. From intake to shipping, every piece of data is now tracked, traceable, and organized.

The result? 60% reduction in manual steps, 100% data centralization, and a scalable structure ready for multiple plants.

**What we learned:** Good software isn't always about the latest frameworks. Sometimes it's about finding the right tool for the job and using it exceptionally well.

---

### **4. Skill x Swap ‚Äì The Skills Marketplace MVP**

**Client:** Sam, Founder (United Kingdom)
**Challenge:** Build a credit-based skill trading marketplace from scratch

This project reminded us why we love building MVPs: taking an idea from concept to working product, fast.

Sam had a clear vision for a marketplace where people trade skills using a credit system. We built the entire MVP in React Native with Firebase, including user onboarding, skill management, matchmaking, and swap request flows‚Äîall in 6 weeks.

The best part? Sam's reaction: "The MVP feels aligned with the exact vision I had in my head."

**What we learned:** MVPs aren't about building everything. They're about building the right things in the right order so the founder can validate the idea and move forward with confidence.

## What We Learned (The Hard Way)

### **1. Clients Don't Buy Code‚ÄîThey Buy Outcomes**

Early on, we'd pitch "React + Node.js + Firebase stack" thinking that's what mattered.

But clients don't care about the tech stack. They care about:
- Will this help me launch faster?
- Will this attract investors?
- Will this make me money?

Now, we lead with outcomes, not technologies.

---

### **2. Saying No is as Important as Saying Yes**

We learned to turn down projects that didn't fit:
- Projects with unrealistic timelines
- Clients who wanted "cheap and fast"
- Vague scopes with no clear goals

Every bad project we said no to made room for a great project we said yes to.

---

### **3. Communication is Half the Product**

The best code in the world doesn't matter if the client doesn't understand what's happening.

We learned to:
- Over-communicate progress
- Explain technical decisions in plain English
- Set expectations clearly and early
- Respond fast (within 24 hours)

This turned clients into advocates.

---

### **4. Specialize, Don't Generalize**

We tried to be "full-stack agency that builds anything."

That didn't work.

Now, we focus on what we're exceptional at:
- SaaS MVPs in 30 days
- React/React Native mobile apps
- AI automation and integrations
- Data-driven dashboards

Specialization = higher quality = better clients = more referrals.

---

### **5. Top Rated Status Isn't Luck‚ÄîIt's a System**

Achieving Top Rated on Upwork wasn't random. It was the result of:
- Delivering on time, every time
- Clear communication throughout
- Going slightly above expectations
- Asking for feedback early
- Treating every project like it matters (because it does)

Excellence isn't an accident. It's a habit.

---

## Mistakes We Made (And What We'd Do Differently)

### **Mistake #1: Underpricing Early Projects**

We were terrified of losing early clients, so we undercharged.

Result? Great portfolio, exhausted team, thin margins.

**What we'd do differently:** Charge what we're worth from day one. The right clients pay for quality.

---

### **Mistake #2: Not Documenting Our Process**

For months, every project felt like starting from scratch.

**What we'd do differently:** Build reusable systems, templates, and workflows early. It compounds.

---

### **Mistake #3: Taking Too Long to Specialize**

We wasted months trying to be everything to everyone.

**What we'd do differently:** Pick a niche, dominate it, then expand.

---

### **Mistake #4: Not Celebrating Wins**

We'd finish a project, ship it, and immediately move to the next one.

**What we'd do differently:** Pause. Celebrate. Reflect. Share the win. It matters.
