## üß≤ HOW TO GUIDE VISITORS TOWARD CONVERSION

### Principle: THE PATH OF LEAST RESISTANCE

```
Awareness ‚Üí Interest ‚Üí Trust ‚Üí Intent ‚Üí Action
```

### Strategy 1: MULTIPLE TOUCHPOINTS
**Don't rely on ONE CTA**

Place conversion opportunities at:
- Top of page (hero)
- Middle of page (after trust building)
- Bottom of page (final CTA)
- Floating widget (always visible)

### Strategy 2: MATCH INTENT TO CTA

**Low Intent Visitors:**
- "Learn More" ‚Üí Articles
- "View Portfolio" ‚Üí Case studies
- "See Our Process" ‚Üí Services

**Medium Intent Visitors:**
- "Get a Quote" ‚Üí Contact
- "Schedule Call" ‚Üí Calendar link
- "View Pricing" ‚Üí Services

**High Intent Visitors:**
- "Start Your Project" ‚Üí Contact
- "Let's Talk" ‚Üí Contact
- "Book Consultation" ‚Üí Calendar

### Strategy 3: REDUCE FRICTION

**Bad Example:**
- 15-field form
- Requires phone number, address, company size
- No indication of what happens next

**Good Example (Current):**
- 6 simple fields (name, email, company, project type, budget, message)
- Clear "Submit" button
- Success message tells them you'll respond in 24 hours

### Strategy 4: BUILD URGENCY (Optional)

**Ethical Urgency Tactics:**
- "Accepting 2 new clients this month"
- "Next available start date: January 15"
- "Limited spots for Q1 2026"

**Never:**
- Fake countdown timers
- False scarcity
- Pushy sales tactics

## üí° WHAT TO DO WHEN A LEAD COMES IN

### STEP 1: RESPOND WITHIN 24 HOURS (Ideally 6 Hours)

**Why:**
- Fast response = professionalism
- They may be talking to competitors
- Strike while interest is hot

**Response Template:**
```
Subject: Re: [Project Type] Inquiry - Zumetrix Labs

Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out! I reviewed your project details and
[personalized comment about their specific needs].

We've successfully delivered [similar project type] for clients
like [relevant client/industry], and I'm confident we can help.

Here's what I suggest as next steps:

1. Schedule a 30-minute discovery call: [calendar link]
2. Share any additional details about [specific question]
3. I'll prepare a preliminary scope and timeline

Are you available [specific day/time] for a quick call?

Best regards,
Zia Hussain
Founder, Zumetrix Labs
[phone number]
[LinkedIn]
```

---

### STEP 2: DISCOVERY CALL (Qualify the Lead)

**Ask:**
- What problem are you trying to solve?
- What's your timeline?
- What's your budget range?
- Who's the decision maker?
- Have you worked with developers before?

**Qualify:**
- ‚úÖ Budget matches your pricing
- ‚úÖ Timeline is realistic
- ‚úÖ Project fits your expertise
- ‚úÖ They seem professional/serious

**Red Flags:**
- ‚ùå "Can you give me a quote first?" (without details)
- ‚ùå Unrealistic budget vs. scope
- ‚ùå "I need it done yesterday"
- ‚ùå "I have no money but we can share equity"

---

### STEP 3: PROPOSAL & PRICING

**Include:**
- Project overview (their goals)
- Scope of work (what you'll build)
- Timeline (with milestones)
- Pricing (clear breakdown)
- Payment terms (50% upfront, 50% on completion)
- Next steps (how to proceed)

**Pricing Strategy:**
- **MVP Development:** $5,000 - $15,000
- **Mobile App:** $10,000 - $30,000
- **AI Automation:** $3,000 - $10,000
- **Custom Web App:** $8,000 - $25,000

**Adjust based on:**
- Complexity
- Timeline pressure
- Client budget
- Your availability

---

### STEP 4: CLOSE THE DEAL

**Contracts:**
- Use a simple service agreement
- Include scope, timeline, payment terms
- Protect yourself (kill clause, IP ownership, revisions)

**Payment:**
- Request 50% upfront (shows commitment)
- Use Upwork/Fiverr for protection (or direct if trusted)
- Track invoices and payments

**Onboarding:**
- Welcome email
- Project kickoff call
- Set expectations clearly
- Provide regular updates
