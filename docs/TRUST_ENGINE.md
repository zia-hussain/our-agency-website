### ‚úÖ Specific Metrics (When Available)

**Good Examples:**
- "50+ projects delivered over 3 years"
- "25+ SaaS MVPs built"
- "Average 4-6 week MVP delivery"

**Bad Examples:**
- "100% satisfaction rate" (unverifiable)

**Rule:** Only use metrics you can actually count and verify

## TIER 5: PROCESS TRANSPARENCY

### ‚úÖ Show Your Actual Work

**High-Trust Actions:**
- Share code samples (when appropriate)
- Show actual project screenshots
- Document your process with specifics
- Be transparent about what you use and why

**Example:**
"Our typical MVP tech stack: React, Node.js, Supabase, Vercel.
Here's why: [specific reasons]"

**Why This Works:**
- Demonstrates real expertise
- Shows you're not hiding anything
- Builds technical credibility

---

### ‚úÖ Honest About Limitations

**Counterintuitive Trust Builder:**
Being honest about what you DON'T do or CAN'T guarantee

**Examples:**
"We're not designers - we build clean UIs, not Dribbble-level work"
"We can't guarantee your product will get funded"
"If your timeline needs 6 months of procurement, we're not the fit"

**Why This Works:**
- Shows self-awareness
- Reduces bad-fit inquiries
- Signals confidence (don't need to be everything to everyone)

## TRUST SIGNALS TO AVOID

### ‚ùå Self-Awarded Badges

- "Best Agency 2025" (says who?)
- "Top 10 Developers" (based on what?)
- Generic "Trusted" badges (not verified)

### ‚ùå Fake Urgency

- "Only 2 spots left this month!" (if not true)
- Countdown timers (manipulative)
- "Limited time offer" (unless actually limited)

### ‚ùå Vague Metrics

- "Thousands of satisfied clients" (you have 50+ projects, not thousands)
- "Millions generated in revenue" (client revenue? Your revenue? Vague.)
- "Award-winning" (which award?)

### ‚ùå Stock Photography

- Generic happy business people
- Fake office photos
- AI-generated faces

### ‚ùå Unverifiable Claims

- "100% satisfaction guaranteed"
- "Best in the industry"
- "World-class service"

**Rule:** If you can't link to proof, don't say it.

## TRUST SIGNAL HIERARCHY

**Most Credible (Use These):**
1. Platform-verified badges (Upwork Top Rated)
2. Real client names + testimonials
3. Specific project outcomes
4. Live product links
5. LinkedIn recommendations
6. Founder credentials (with links)

**Less Credible (Use Carefully):**
1. Self-reported metrics (if specific and verifiable)
2. Generic statistics (if backed by data)
3. Industry comparisons (if fair and honest)

**Not Credible (Avoid):**
1. Self-awarded badges
2. Vague claims
3. Fake urgency
4. Unverifiable superlatives
